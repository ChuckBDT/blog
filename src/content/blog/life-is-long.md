I can't sleep tonight. Too many thoughts.

The air bed I sleep on just broke, again (third one in four months). I'm so tired of the renovation work, I can't wait to go back home.

Anyway, just a little story I experienced yesterday that I wanted to share with you. 

I was eating lunch with some friends in their 40s, some of them are Russian. I don't remember how, but the conversation turned to nightclubs among other things. As I simply said "That's not really my scene, I just get bored in nightclubs", my friend told me this:  

"When I was young, around my 20s, I lived in Saint Petersburg and man, I was spending time in nightclubs. During that time I used to breakdance and sometimes had dance battles with people."

I don't want to say too much about this friend to respect his privacy, but let's just say that if you look at him today, you can easily see that he's in great shape and still practicing sports. But there's no way you could imagine him breakdancing in a nightclub. Absolutely no way.

I mean, it's so far from where he is and what he does today, it's like another person or another life.

And that made me realize this: __Life is long.__

We — at least I — easily get anxious during the bad or "empty" times of life. It feels like time is passing by, like the situation we're in is forever, with no way out. But that's wrong.

Once again, privacy for this friend so I can't be explicit, but the guy is a fighter. He never gives up, no matter the obstacles. And I think that's a lesson for everyone:

As long as you keep moving forward, fighting, believing, anything is possible.

Maybe you don't like the situation you're facing right now — that happens to everyone. Don't give up. Find or make your way out.

Serious things take time but life is long enough to give you the opportunity to live several of them.

And even if you're not experiencing hard times in your life right now, I think that the idea that you can be a totally different person in 10 years is really exciting, right? Who will you be? Who will I be? We will see!

I hope this has inspired one of you, going back to bed now. I've got work tomorrow.

See ya!
