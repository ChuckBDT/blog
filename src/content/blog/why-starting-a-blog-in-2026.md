Since a few months, i'm really concerned about the way we think / do things.

Every time i do something "automatically" and by that i mean without thinking first, as it always had been the only way to do, i stop.

Why ? Why do i check my mails every time i touch my phone ? Why do i choose this store / buy this product instead of another ? Why do i check Instagram even if i know i will fell on content i don't really want to see ?

And more important to me: How much time do these reflexes take? What's really the output quality ?

Even if i don't have the full answer yet, i'm approaching it.
## The fail of social networks
For me, it's obvious now that the social networks have taken a totally different way that what they were made for. 

I mean, it's not by scrolling, liking, sharing photos of your friends / family members or strangers that you really connect to them, know them.

Every social network is made to keep you as long as possible on the platform and for this, you need to consume the information, not think about it. And that's the worst for me. 

I'm not even talking about TikTok, YouTube Shorts, Instagram Reels.

## The impact on our health
I like to watch and listen to the [Diary of a CEO](https://www.youtube.com/TheDiaryOfACEO).

The host has a calm voice and does not interrupt the guests, that's really appreciated.

As on other YT channels / podcasts, we should not take what's said for the absolute truth but one thing all the speakers are saying is simple : Our brain is not made to treat as much informations.

And to be honest, just a little introspection and you will feel it. We don't need a "specialist" to feel that. 

Next time you spend what you think is a small time on social networks, when you're done take thirty seconds and just try to remember what you watched. Try also to remember what happened around you during the time you watched this content. And more important, try to qualify the real time you just spent.

You'll see that this "quick" felt like 2 minutes but it actually took 10, you remember only one or two posts / pictures you saw and you have no idea what happened around you.

One useful information : All the silicon valley engineers that create and maintain the social networks use them as less as possible and strictly forbid their child to use them.

I mean, what is a product that a group of people build, never consume or give to their relatives, get money with, get the users addicted and is proved to be bad for the brain's health ? 

It pretty much looks like a drug to me.
## A positive angle
Even if the way i write my observations seems negative: it is not. It's the opposite actually and this blog is the proof.

I think we always have the choice. The power to choose what we want to do with our precious time. 

For me now, it won't be scrolling as an addict to see the arranged life of people i don't even care about. Checking mails every hour even if i'm not expecting something important to arrive. 

From now on, i will take time, to write this blog, to read other people's blogs, to think.

And if i need to connect with my siblings, why not simply meet / call them?

Quality other quantity.
